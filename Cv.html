Academic Experience

Systems Programming for ARM (University of York, Jan 2016 – Jan 2017)
A fourth-year module, which involved implementing a multi-threaded operating system on to the ARM core M3, STM32F4 development board. The features implemented include a priority scheduler, mutex, counting semaphores, inter-task communication, MMU, FPU and a protected memory pool.
Design and Construction Module (University of York, Jan 2016 – March 2016)
A third year module, which involved teams of five to design and construct an environment-monitoring system. The system was specifically designed for animal transportation. The hardware sensors (temperature, humidity, light levels, CO2 levels, camera etc.) would be placed in the trailer, which would interface with an STM32F4 microcontroller and send the sensor’s data to another microcontroller via XBEE. The second microcontroller- located in the driver’s cabin- would provide a user interface on an LCD touchscreen and display the data, as well as giving some configuration options. This data would also be recorded on an SD card, and could then be exported to display automated graphs in excel.
UKESF Summer workshop (University of York, Sep 2014 – Sep 2014)
Attended the ‘UK Electronics Skills Foundation’ (UKESF) Summer Workshop as part of a 3 year scholarship. This covered non-technical skills such as strategy and leadership, project management, communication skills, negotiation skills, lean thinking for design and manufacturing, and business ethics.
